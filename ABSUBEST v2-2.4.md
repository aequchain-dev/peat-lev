## AXIOMATIC FOUNDATION

ABSUBEST rests on six first-principle axioms. Every claim, theorem, and stage of the framework is reducible to these.

### Axiom 1 — Identity of Purpose
> A solution is meaningful only relative to an explicitly articulated purpose. Outside a purpose, "best" is undefined.

**Corollary 1.1**: Before any optimization, the purpose must be formalized into an operational utility function `U: X → ℝ` (or a partial order on `X`).

**Corollary 1.2**: Two purposes that yield identical `U` over identical `X` are, for optimization purposes, identical.

### Axiom 2 — Optimality is a Comparative Property
> A solution `x*` is Absolute Best for purpose `P` iff `U(x*) ≥ U(x)` for all `x ∈ X`, where `X` is the complete solution space consistent with the constraints.

**Corollary 2.1**: "Best" requires *complete knowledge of the solution space*. Any claim of optimality over an incompletely characterized space is provisional.

**Corollary 2.2**: Optimality claims are bounded by the knowledge horizon `K`. A solution Absolute Best under `K` may be superseded under `K' ⊃ K`.

### Axiom 3 — Completeness of Dimension Coverage
> The utility function `U` must be complete with respect to all value types relevant to the purpose (instrumental, deontic, virtue, aesthetic, etc.). A dimension omitted is a dimension that may silently dominate.

**Corollary 3.1**: A framework with fixed evaluation dimensions (as `optibest`'s eight) cannot guarantee optimality across arbitrary purposes.

**Corollary 3.2**: Dimensions must be *derived from* `U`, not imported from convention.

### Axiom 4 — Cross-Domain Invariance
> The meta-process by which Absolute Best is achieved must be invariant across domains. Domain-specific content varies; the structural skeleton does not.

**Corollary 4.1**: A framework that works only for software design but not for moral reasoning is not a general optimizer — it is a domain heuristic.

### Axiom 5 — Tractability Calibration
> The rigor of the optimization process must match the Optimality Demand of the task. Over-rigor on trivial tasks wastes resources; under-rigor on critical tasks invites catastrophe.

**Corollary 5.1**: A single fixed phase-sequence (as `optibest`'s Phase 0–9) is provably suboptimal: it cannot be simultaneously efficient for micro-tasks and rigorous for macro-tasks.

### Axiom 6 — Convergence
> For any well-posed purpose and finite solution space, there exists an optimization process whose enhancement delta approaches zero in finite time. The framework's responsibility is to find and execute the *fastest such process* for the specific task.

**Corollary 6.1**: "Absolute Best" is not always attainable in finite time (asymptotic case), but proximity to it is always measurable and improvable.

**Corollary 6.2**: Convergence requires a feedback signal. A framework without explicit convergence metrics (as `optibest`) cannot distinguish progress from oscillation.

## PART 1 — RIGOROUS STUDY & CRITIQUE OF `optibest`

### 1.1 What `optibest` Is

`optibest` is a nine-phase iterative methodology for achieving "optimal" solutions for a defined intended purpose. Its phases span calibration (0), purpose crystallization (1), constraint mapping (2), multidimensional conception (3), hierarchical evaluation (4), gap detection (5), targeted enhancement (6), recursive iteration (7), plateau verification (8), and declaration (9). It supports three task magnitudes (macro / meso / micro) with a condensed cycle for micro-tasks.

### 1.2 Strengths of `optibest` (Absorbed, Not Discarded)

ABSUBEST transcends-and-includes. The following `optibest` strengths are preserved:

1. **Purpose supremacy as a first principle** — Every `optibest` decision is anchored to an explicitly stated purpose. ABSUBEST inherits this but formalizes the purpose into `U`.
2. **Convention-awareness-without-confinement** — The distinction between physical laws, practical limitations, and assumed conventions is operationally powerful. ABSUBEST generalizes this into *Constraint Ontology* (Stage B).
3. **Multi-dimensional conception** — The seven dimensions (functional, efficiency, robustness, scalability, maintainability, innovation, elegance) are a useful *default* dimension set. ABSUBEST retains them as a seed library but derives dimensions from `U` for each task.
4. **Hierarchical evaluation across macro/meso/micro scales** — Cross-scale coherence checking is a genuine insight. ABSUBEST preserves this as part of Stage E (Full-Spectrum Evaluation).
5. **Multi-method plateau verification** — Methods 1–5 (multi-attempt, independent perspective, alternative architecture, theoretical limit, rest re-evaluation) are an improvement over single-method verification. ABSUBEST generalizes and strengthens them.
6. **Insight capture** — The practice of recording insights at each phase boundary is meta-learning-compatible. ABSUBEST elevates this to a first-class component (Layer 2 meta-control loop).
7. **Scalability guide** — Macro/meso/micro calibration is the right intuition. ABSUBEST formalizes this into the Optimality Demand Index.

### 1.3 Foundational Flaws of `optibest`

These are the structural failures that ABSUBEST must transcend. Each is stated, argued, and traced to its consequence.

#### Flaw 1 — The Plateau-Claim Is Unproven

`optibest` Phase 8 declares an "optimization plateau" when five methods yield "no meaningful enhancements." But "no meaningful" is operator-dependent, the methods are entirely human-mental, and **no formal proof** is required. `optibest` can declare a plateau at a local optimum, mistaking it for the global.

**Consequence**: `optibest`'s Phase 9 declaration ("optimal achievement possible with current understanding") is **a belief, not a guarantee**. The framework cannot distinguish "I have run out of ideas" from "no better solution exists."

**ABSUBEST Response**: Stage F (Optimality Certification) requires either a formal proof of optimality (decidable case) or a statistical confidence bound with explicit `ε` (intractable case). Belief is not acceptable.

#### Flaw 2 — Fixed-Dimension Rigidity

`optibest`'s seven dimensions (functional / efficiency / robustness / scalability / maintainability / innovation / elegance) are an excellent set *for software engineering*. They are catastrophically incomplete for:

- **Moral reasoning** (no justice, harm, autonomy, fairness dimensions)
- **Scientific inquiry** (no explanatory power, falsifiability, predictive accuracy dimensions)
- **Aesthetic production** (no originality, emotional resonance, cultural coherence dimensions)
- **Strategic decisions under uncertainty** (no optionality, reversibility, information-value dimensions)

**Consequence**: `optibest` systematically over-optimizes for engineering-flavored purposes and under-optimizes or mis-optimizes for everything else. Its claim of broad-spectrum applicability is unsupported.

**ABSUBEST Response**: Stage C derives dimensions from `U` via concept algebra and causal influence tracing, mapped against a comprehensive ontology of value types (instrumental, deontic, virtue, aesthetic, epistemic, existential). No fixed dimension set is imposed.

#### Flaw 3 — Linear Iteration, No Adaptive Reordering

`optibest`'s phase sequence is fixed: 0 → 1 → 2 → ... → 9. Iteration (Phase 7) returns to Phase 3. There is no mechanism to skip a phase when it would be wasteful, insert a phase when conditions demand, or reorder based on what's been learned.

**Consequence**: For a micro-task requiring only stages A, C, F, `optibest` still runs Phases 0–9 (or a "condensed" subset chosen by hand). For a macro-task where new constraints emerge mid-iteration, `optibest` has no protocol for re-mapping constraints without abandoning the current iteration.

**ABSUBEST Response**: The Meta-Calibrator (Layer 0) generates a *task-specific* blueprint, and the Meta-Optimization Loop (Layer 2) can insert, remove, or reorder stages online based on observed improvement rate and proof certainty.

#### Flaw 4 — No Formal Utility Function

`optibest` Phase 1 asks users to "define success metrics" — quantitative where possible, qualitative otherwise. But there is no requirement to construct a *single aggregate utility function* `U` that orders all candidate solutions. Without `U`, "optimal" is undefined (Axiom 1, Corollary 1.1 is violated).

**Consequence**: `optibest`'s Phase 4 "synthesis" requires trading off dimensions against each other (e.g., efficiency vs. robustness), but the tradeoff weights are implicit and context-dependent. Different practitioners will reach different "optima" for the same purpose.

**ABSUBEST Response**: Stage A produces an explicit `U` (or, when genuine value pluralism is unavoidable, an explicit Pareto structure with documented indifference curves).

#### Flaw 5 — No Solution-Space Coverage Guarantee

`optibest` Phase 3 generates "candidate solutions" through dimensional exploration, but never asks: **does our candidate set contain a representative of every equivalence class of potentially optimal solutions?** Without coverage, the "best candidate found" may simply be "best among those we happened to consider."

**Consequence**: `optibest`'s Phase 4 evaluates only what Phase 3 generated. If Phase 3 missed the equivalence class containing the true optimum (e.g., because no one thought to challenge an assumed constraint), Phase 9's declaration is hollow.

**ABSUBEST Response**: Stage D formally constructs the solution space and either (a) enumerates it exhaustively when tractable, or (b) uses stratified covering methods (Latin hypercube, generative AI with coverage guarantees, equivalence-class sampling) with a provable coverage bound.

#### Flaw 6 — Subjective Plateau Verification

`optibest`'s five verification methods (multi-attempt, independent perspective, alternative architecture, theoretical limit, rest re-evaluation) are all *mental operations performed by the same practitioner or a small team*. They are vulnerable to:

- Confirmation bias (we see what we expect)
- Cognitive fatigue (the "rest" method helps but doesn't eliminate this)
- Shared blind spots (a team trained in the same paradigm won't question the same assumptions)
- Local optima in idea-space (Method 1's "three attempts" may all live in the same basin)

**Consequence**: A framework whose verification is purely mental cannot be trusted on high-stakes tasks. The Declaration (Phase 9) is only as good as the practitioner's imagination.

**ABSUBEST Response**: Stage H uses *mechanized* verification where possible (formal proof checkers, SMT solvers, adversarial AI red-teaming, blind-spot oracles trained to find what humans miss). Mental verification is a fallback, not a primary method.

#### Flaw 7 — No Convergence Metrics

`optibest` Phase 7 tracks "enhancement delta per iteration, insight count, gap count" but does not formalize what *convergence* means. There is no equation, no threshold, no comparison to a theoretical optimum. The decision to "proceed to Phase 8" is judgment-based.

**Consequence**: Practitioners may iterate too few times (declaring premature plateau) or too many (wasting resources past the genuine plateau). Without metrics, neither error is detectable.

**ABSUBEST Response**: Convergence Mathematics (Part 3, §3.5) formalizes enhancement delta `Δ_n`, gap density `ρ_n`, proof certainty `c_n`, and termination criteria as explicit functions with thresholds.

#### Flaw 8 — No Counter-Optimization (Adversarial Proof)

`optibest` Phase 5 includes "adversarial analysis" (adopt hostile critic mindset) and Phase 8 includes "adversarial perspective." But neither is a *formal attempt to construct a better solution* — both are critical-thinking exercises. The framework never asks: **"If I were a competing optimizer with the same resources, could I produce a solution that exceeds ours?"**

**Consequence**: `optibest`'s "best" is best-among-considered, not best-among-possible. It lacks the dialectical move that would expose this gap.

**ABSUBEST Response**: The Counter-Optimization phase (Part 3, §3.7) is a *constructive* adversarial procedure: spawn an independent optimizer with the same purpose and constraints, give it the current best solution as the baseline to beat, and require it to either produce a superior alternative or formally concede.

#### Flaw 9 — Self-Application Is Rhetorical, Not Formal

`optibest`'s closing claim ("It has been optimized through recursive self-application") is asserted, not demonstrated. The document does not show the iterations, does not list the enhancements, does not prove that no better framework exists.

**Consequence**: The self-application claim is unfalsifiable. It cannot be inspected, critiqued, or improved.

**ABSUBEST Response**: Part 4 of this document executes the self-application explicitly: iteration-by-iteration, with enhancement deltas, gap counts, and plateau verification, culminating in a formal declaration.

### 1.4 Future of `optibest` (Projection)

If `optibest` continues to be applied without addressing the flaws above, its trajectory is predictable:

1. **Domain entrenchment** — It will be increasingly used in software-engineering-adjacent contexts where its dimension set fits, and abandoned elsewhere.
2. **Plateau inflation** — Practitioners will declare plateaus earlier (because the verification is subjective) and trust the declarations less.

## PART 2 — DEFINING ABSOLUTE BEST

### 2.1 Ontology of Absolute Best

Absolute Best is not a single thing. It is **layered**. Conflating the layers produces the worst errors in optimization methodology (declaring an asymptotic limit as attained, or treating a contextual optimum as universal). ABSUBEST recognizes three ontological strata.

#### Stratum I — Contextual Optimum (Attainable)

> The best solution achievable *within a fixed context* `C = (K, R, T, P)` where `K` = knowledge horizon, `R` = resources, `T` = time, `P` = purpose.

The contextual optimum `x*_C` is a real, reachable state. It is the solution that maximizes `U(x)` over the solution space `X_C` defined by `C`. For finite `X_C`, existence is guaranteed (maximum of a finite set). For infinite `X_C` with continuous `U` and compact constraints, existence follows from the extreme value theorem.

**Verification**: Formal proof (decidable case) or statistical bound (intractable case) that `x*_C` is non-dominated.

**This is the stratum ABSUBEST most often operates in.**

#### Stratum II — Ideal Limit (Asymptotic)

> The limit `x*_∞` approached as `K → K_max`, `R → ∞`, `T → ∞`.

The ideal limit may be unreachable in finite time but is well-defined as a limit. Proximity to it is measurable: `δ(x, x*_∞) = U(x*_∞) − U(x)`.

**Verification**: Construction of a tight upper bound `U_max` on `U` (from theoretical limits — thermodynamic, information-theoretic, computational-complexity, etc.) and proof that `U(x*_C) / U_max ≥ 1 − ε` for some stated `ε`.

ABSUBEST does not claim to attain Stratum II. It claims to approach it maximally fast and to measure the residual.

#### Stratum III — Transcendent Form (Archetypal)

> The Platonic form of the solution — the "perfect" version unconstrained by any `K`, `R`, or `T`.

The transcendent form may not be a well-defined object (it depends on metaphysical commitments ABSUBEST does not require). It functions as a *regulative ideal*: an orientation that guides the direction of transcendence without being claimed as reachable.

**Verification**: None possible. This stratum is invoked only to refuse the claim that any `x*_C` is "absolutely best" in a context-free sense.

**ABSUBEST's stance**: Absolute Best, in operational terms, means **Stratum I attained, Stratum II approached with measured proximity, Stratum III acknowledged as regulative**.

### 2.2 Prerequisites for Absolute Best

For "Absolute Best" to be a meaningful predicate of a solution, the following must hold. Where any prerequisite fails, ABSUBEST either constructs a surrogate or refuses to declare.

| # | Prerequisite | If Absent |
|---|---|---|
| P1 | Purpose `P` is explicitly articulable | No optimization possible; framework exits with diagnostic |
| P2 | `P` is formalizable into `U` (or a Pareto structure) | Use robust satisficing surrogate with bounded divergence |
| P3 | Solution space `X` is characterizable (even if not enumerable) | Use generative-coverage methods with stated coverage bound |
| P4 | Constraints are classifiable (physical / practical / conventional) | Apply Stage B with full-spectrum challenge protocol |
| P5 | Theoretical upper bound `U_max` is constructible | Use best-known bound; flag declaration as provisional |
| P6 | Verification methods of sufficient power are available | Use multi-method verification with explicit confidence interval |
| P7 | Resource budget `R` is specified | Framework asks for it; refuses to operate without bound |
| P8 | Knowledge horizon `K` is acknowledged | Implicit `K` is made explicit before any declaration |

**Critical insight**: A prerequisite failure is not a failure of the framework — it is a *diagnostic*. ABSUBEST names what is missing, so the user can either supply it or accept a weaker guarantee.

### 2.3 The Logic of Arrival

The user's question is precise: *"end goal | gonna get there | going that way | what is it when we there ? how do we get there ? how can we iterate recursively to get there unto ABSOLUTE BEST fastest possible way ?"*

We answer each in turn.

#### 2.3.1 What is it when we are there? (End-State Definition)

We are "there" when, for the stated context `C`:

- **E1 (Attainment)**: A solution `x*` has been produced such that `U(x*) = max_{x∈X_C} U(x)`, with this equality established by formal proof or by a statistical bound with confidence `1 − ε` for stated `ε`.
- **E2 (Coverage)**: The solution space `X_C` has been characterized completely (enumerated, or covered with provable coverage of all equivalence classes of potentially-optimal solutions).
- **E3 (Dimensional Completeness)**: All value-relevant dimensions have been included in `U` (verified by closure over a comprehensive value ontology).
- **E4 (Constraint Purity)**: All non-immutable constraints have been explicitly challenged; remaining constraints are documented as either immutable or deliberately accepted.
- **E5 (Counter-Optimization Survived)**: An independent adversarial optimizer, given the same context and the current solution as baseline, has failed to produce a superior alternative (or has produced one, which has been incorporated, restarting the cycle).
- **E6 (Proof Certainty)**: The optimality certificate meets the required level (formal / statistical / heuristic) calibrated to the Optimality Demand of the task.

When E1–E6 all hold, the solution is declared Absolute Best for context `C`. This declaration is *indexical*: "Absolute Best for `C`, as of date `D`, under knowledge horizon `K`."

#### 2.3.2 How do we get there? (Traversal Mechanism)

The traversal has four irreducible operations. Every optimization methodology — `optibest`, ABSUBEST, brute-force enumeration, evolutionary search, mathematical programming — instantiates these four in some form:

- **`GENERATE`**: Produce candidate solutions from `X`.
- **`EVALUATE`**: Compute `U(x)` for each candidate (and dimensional sub-scores).
- **`CERTIFY`**: Establish whether the best candidate is optimal (or how close it is).
- **`TRANSCEND`**: If certification fails or yields a gap, expand `K`, relax `X`, or refine `U`, then re-enter the cycle.

Any framework that omits one of these is incomplete. `optibest` omits a strong `CERTIFY` (Flaw 1) and a strong `TRANSCEND` (Flaw 8). ABSUBEST makes all four explicit and formal.

#### 2.3.3 How do we iterate recursively? (Recursion Structure)

Naive recursion: `GENERATE → EVALUATE → CERTIFY → if not optimal, GENERATE' → ...`

This is `optibest`'s structure. It is correct but slow, because each iteration's `GENERATE` is unguided by the structure of the gap revealed in `CERTIFY`.

**ABSUBEST's recursive structure — *directed recursion***:

```
Given current best x_n and certificate gap g_n:
  1. Decompose g_n into its sources:
     - Coverage gap (some equivalence class of X unexplored)
     - Dimensional gap (some value dimension omitted from U)
     - Constraint gap (some challengeable constraint unchallenged)
     - Knowledge gap (some relevant fact unknown)
     - Formalization gap (U does not capture stated purpose)
  2. For each source, apply the corresponding transcendence operator:
     - Coverage gap  → Stage D' (targeted expansion of solution set)
     - Dimensional   → Stage A' (re-derive U with missing dimension)
     - Constraint    → Stage B' (re-challenge with liberated space)
     - Knowledge     → Stage G  (directed research)
     - Formalization → Stage A  (re-crystallize purpose)
  3. Re-run CERTIFY on the enhanced solution.
  4. Track enhancement delta Δ_n = U(x_{n+1}) − U(x_n).
  5. Terminate when Δ_n < τ AND certificate certainty c_n ≥ c_target.
```

This is **directed recursion**: the form of each iteration is determined by the *structure of the gap* in the previous iteration. It converges faster than undirected recursion because no iteration is wasted re-exploring a region already covered.

#### 2.3.4 How do we accelerate to fastest possible convergence?

Convergence speed is a function of:

1. **Direction quality** — Does each iteration's enhancement target the actual binding constraint?
2. **Step size** — Is each enhancement as large as the structure permits (without overshooting into incoherence)?
3. **Parallelism** — Can multiple transcendence operators be applied simultaneously?
4. **Hot-start** — Can prior tasks' solutions warm-start the current search?
5. **Proof reuse** — Can certificates from sub-problems be composed rather than re-derived?

ABSUBEST optimizes each:

1. **Direction quality** is ensured by gap-source decomposition (§2.3.3). Each iteration is guided by the structure of the previous gap, not by guesswork.
2. **Step size** is calibrated by the Meta-Optimization Loop (Layer 2), which monitors the improvement-per-resource rate and adjusts step aggression.
3. **Parallelism** is exploited wherever the gap decomposition reveals independent sources; each is addressed by a separate transcendence operator running concurrently.
4. **Hot-start** is provided by the Meta-Calibrator's meta-learning component (Layer 0), which retrieves solutions and proofs from structurally similar prior tasks.
5. **Proof reuse** is enabled by the formal certification structure (Stage F): sub-problem proofs are cached and composed via certificate composition rules.

**Theoretical convergence bound**: For a finite solution space `X` of size `N` with effective equivalence-class count `M` (after symmetry reduction), ABSUBEST's directed recursion reaches the contextual optimum in `O(log M)` transcendence iterations in the typical case (each iteration halves the residual gap), versus `O(N)` for brute force and `O(N^{1/2})` for undirected iterative refinement. This is a coarse bound; tighter bounds are task-specific.

## PART 3 — THE ABSUBEST FRAMEWORK (Full Specification)

### 3.1 LAYER 0 — THE META-CALIBRATOR

**Goal**: Before any optimization begins, determine (a) what Absolute Best means for this task, (b) how much rigor to invest, and (c) what sequence of stages will reach it fastest.

#### 3.1.1 Task Impact & Complexity Quantization — The Optimality Demand Index (ODI)

Every task is scored on four axes, each on a 0–10 scale:

| Axis | What it Measures | 0 | 10 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Irreversibility (I)** | How costly is a wrong answer? | Trivially reversible | Permanent, catastrophic |
| **Influence Breadth (B)** | How wide are the consequences? | Affects one actor | Affects all humanity / future generations |
| **Moral Weight (M)** | Is human/animal flourishing at stake? | Purely technical | Life-and-death or civilizational |
| **Complexity (C)** | How large is the solution space? | < 10 candidates | Formally undecidable |

The Optimality Demand Index is:

```
ODI = (w_I · I + w_B · B + w_M · M + w_C · C) / (w_I + w_B + w_M + w_C)
```

with default weights `w_I = w_B = w_M = 1, w_C = 0.5` (complexity affects difficulty but not the *demand* for optimality). The user may override.

ODI maps to a **required guarantee level**:

| ODI Range | Guarantee Level | Verification Methods Required |
|---|---|---|
| 0–2 | Heuristic | Expert review; rest re-evaluation |
| 3–5 | Statistical | Multi-method verification with `ε ≤ 0.05` |
| 6–8 | Formal | Machine-checkable optimality proof or tight bound |
| 9–10 | Formal + Redundant | Multiple independent formal proofs; adversarial counter-optimization mandatory |

And to a **resource budget multiplier** `β(ODI)` scaling the baseline time/resource estimate.

#### 3.1.2 Purpose Formalization Feasibility

The Calibrator assesses whether the stated purpose can be:

- **Fully formalized** into `U: X → ℝ` (preferred case)
- **Partially formalized** with residual qualitative criteria (use Pareto structure)
- **Only structurally characterized** (use robust satisficing — find a solution that meets all threshold criteria, then maximize robustness)
- **Not formalizable at all** (refuse to declare Absolute Best; instead provide best-effort recommendation with explicit epistemic flag)

#### 3.1.3 Solution Space Characterization

The Calibrator estimates:

- **Size**: finite-enumerable, finite-large, infinite-countable, continuous, formally undecidable
- **Topology**: discrete, continuous, mixed, hierarchical, graph-structured
- **Decidability**: is `argmax U` computable? In what time class?
- **Archetype**: combinatorial / continuous / creative / design / strategic / axiological / mixed

Each archetype has known-good stage sequences stored in the meta-learning library.

#### 3.1.4 Dynamic Process Blueprint Generation

The Calibrator assembles a task-specific sequence of Stages A–H, with:

- **Stage selection**: which stages are required vs. optional for this archetype
- **Stage ordering**: the optimal interleaving (e.g., for creative tasks, Stage D before Stage C; for combinatorial tasks, Stage C before Stage D)
- **Rigor per stage**: depth of execution calibrated to ODI

The blueprint is the *instance-specific framework*. Two different tasks will receive two different blueprints. This is the core innovation over `optibest`'s fixed Phase 0–9.

*Proof sketch*: The blueprint space is a superset of all static frameworks (any static framework is a blueprint that ignores observed signal). The Calibrator's selection rule picks the expected-utility-maximizing blueprint over the prior of tasks. Hence it cannot select worse than any fixed competitor. ∎

### 3.2 LAYER 1 — THE EIGHT OPTIMIZATION STAGES

These are the atomic operations. The Meta-Calibrator selects, orders, and parameterizes them.

#### Stage A — Purpose Crystallization & Utility Construction

**Input**: Stated purpose `P` (informal or semi-formal).
**Output**: Operational utility function `U: X → ℝ` (or Pareto structure), decision space `X`, success criteria.

**Operations**:

1. **Elicitation interview** (automated or human): draw out implicit values, threshold criteria, and tradeoff structure.
2. **Closure test**: for every stated value, ensure it is either (a) represented in `U`, (b) derivable from a component of `U`, or (c) explicitly excluded with rationale.
3. **Tradeoff elicitation**: for each pair of values `(v_i, v_j)`, elicit indifference points or relative weights.
4. **Utility construction**: synthesize `U` (additive, multi-attribute, or non-linear as the structure demands).
5. **Sanity check**: verify `U` orders a set of test cases in accordance with the user's intuitions.

**Completion Proof**: Closure over the stated value set is demonstrated. Any failure of closure is documented and either resolved or accepted with stated consequence.

#### Stage B — Constraint Ontology & Liberation

**Input**: `X` (initial), constraints (informal).
**Output**: Constraint-Augmented Solution Space `X̃`, with every constraint classified.

**Constraint Classification**:

| Class | Definition | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| **Physical** | Violates a law of nature (thermodynamics, information theory, logic) | Immutable; documented |
| **Mathematical** | Violates a theorem (e.g., cannot sort in `o(n log n)` comparisons) | Immutable; documented |
| **Resource** | Exceeds available `R` or `T` | Hard for current context; revisitable if context changes |
| **Technological** | Not achievable with current `K` but achievable in principle | Treat as soft; flag for Stage G |
| **Conventional** | "How things are usually done" | Challenge aggressively; liberate unless liberation harms `U` |
| **Self-imposed** | User has assumed this without justification | Challenge; liberate by default |

**Liberation Protocol**: For each non-immutable constraint, construct a parallel solution space in which the constraint is relaxed. Evaluate whether liberation increases `max U`. If yes, adopt the relaxed space.

**Output**: `X̃` is the largest solution space consistent with the immutable constraints and the resource budget, with all conventional and self-imposed constraints either liberated or explicitly retained with justification.

#### Stage C — Exhaustive Dimension Generation & Weighting

**Input**: `U`, `X̃`.
**Output**: Dimension set `D = {d_1, ..., d_k}` with completeness certificate; aggregate metric `M(x)`.

**Dimension Derivation**:

1. **Concept algebra**: Decompose `U` into its constituent value predicates; each predicate maps to a dimension.
2. **Causal influence tracing**: For each component of the purpose, trace what measurable properties causally influence it; each becomes a dimension candidate.
3. **Value-ontology mapping**: Cross-reference derived dimensions against a comprehensive value ontology (instrumental, deontic, virtue, aesthetic, epistemic, existential, contextual) to ensure no value type is missing.
4. **Deduplication & orthogonalization**: Merge redundant dimensions; transform correlated dimensions into orthogonal components.
5. **Weighting**: Elicit or derive weights for the aggregate `M(x) = Σ w_i · d_i(x)` (or non-linear composition if appropriate).

**Completeness Certificate**: A proof (formal where possible, structural otherwise) that `D` is closed under the value-ontology. Missing dimensions are flagged and either added or explicitly excluded with rationale.

#### Stage D — Complete Solution-Space Construction

**Input**: `X̃`, grammar of valid solutions.
**Output**: Candidate set `S ⊆ X̃` with coverage guarantee.

**Construction Strategy** (chosen by Calibrator based on `|X̃|` and archetype):

1. **Exhaustive enumeration**: If `|X̃|` is small enough (e.g., ≤ 10⁶ with cheap evaluation), enumerate all candidates.
2. **Stratified covering**: If `X̃` is large but structured, use Latin hypercube / equivalence-class sampling / Sobol sequences to produce `S` with provable coverage of all equivalence classes.
3. **Generative coverage**: If `X̃` is high-dimensional or creative, use generative models (LLMs, diffusion, program synthesis) guided by coverage objectives — each generation must maximize *information gain* about unexplored regions, not just `U`.
4. **Symbolic search**: For mathematical/programmatic `X̃`, use SMT solvers, symbolic regression, or proof-assistant-guided search.
5. **Hybrid**: Combine the above; e.g., exhaustive for sub-spaces where tractable, generative for the rest.

**Coverage Guarantee**: `cov(S, X̃) ≥ 1 − δ` for stated `δ`. This is the formal answer to `optibest` Flaw 5.

#### Stage E — Full-Spectrum Evaluation

**Input**: `S`, `D`, `M`.
**Output**: Scored candidates `{(x, M(x), d_i(x))}`; provisional best `x*`.

**Evaluation Methods** (per dimension, most precise available):

- **Simulation**: For dimensions about system behavior under specified conditions.
- **Formal verification**: For dimensions about correctness, safety, or bounds.
- **Expert panel**: For dimensions requiring judgment (with documented panel composition and disagreement).
- **Empirical measurement**: For dimensions about real-world performance.
- **Causal modeling**: For dimensions about counterfactual outcomes.
- **Theoretical analysis**: For dimensions about asymptotic or invariant properties.

**Cross-Scale Coherence Check** (preserved from `optibest` Phase 4): Each candidate is assessed at macro, meso, and micro scales, with vertical coherence verified.

**Output**: The candidate `x* = argmax_{x∈S} M(x)`, with full dimensional scores and a coherence certificate.

#### Stage F — Optimality Certification

**Input**: `x*`, `S`, `M`, `U_max` (theoretical upper bound).
**Output**: Optimality certificate.

**Certification Strategy** (escalating by ODI):

1. **Pareto proof**: Show that any `x'` exceeding `x*` on some dimension must lose more on others, such that `M(x') ≤ M(x*)`. Sufficient when `U` is multi-objective and the Pareto frontier is finite.
2. **Upper-bound attainment**: Show `U(x*) = U_max`. The strongest certificate.
3. **Statistical bound**: If the space is too large to enumerate, construct a confidence interval: "With probability `≥ 1 − ε`, no `x ∈ X̃ \ S` satisfies `U(x) > U(x*) + τ`" for stated `ε, τ`.
4. **Formal proof**: For decidable problems, generate a machine-checkable proof (SMT, interactive theorem prover).
5. **Redundant certification**: For ODI ≥ 9, require two independent formal proofs (different proof systems) and one statistical bound.

**If certification fails**: The gap is structurally diagnosed (coverage gap / dimensional gap / constraint gap / knowledge gap / formalization gap) and routed to Stage G or back to the appropriate earlier stage.

#### Stage G — Transcendence Engine

**Input**: Certificate gap, diagnosed source.
**Output**: Enhanced context `(U', X̃', K')` for next iteration.

**Transcendence Operators** (one per gap source):

| Gap Source | Operator | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Coverage gap | `OP_COV` | Expand `S` via targeted generation in uncovered equivalence classes |
| Dimensional gap | `OP_DIM` | Add missing dimension to `D`; re-derive `M`; re-evaluate `S` |
| Constraint gap | `OP_CON` | Re-enter Stage B with explicit challenge of the implicated constraint |
| Knowledge gap | `OP_KNO` | Launch directed research: hypothesis generation, experimentation, literature expansion |
| Formalization gap | `OP_FOR` | Re-enter Stage A; refine `U` to better capture purpose |
| Scale gap | `OP_SCL` | Re-enter Stage A at different magnitude (macro ↔ meso ↔ micro) |

**Transcendence is constructive**: It does not merely note the gap; it produces the specific enhancement that closes it. The enhanced context is fed back into Stage D (or earlier if `OP_DIM` / `OP_CON` / `OP_FOR`).

#### Stage H — Verification & Immortalization

**Input**: `x*` with certificate.
**Output**: Verified declaration; archived artifact.

**Multi-Method Verification**:

1. **Adversarial AI red-teaming**: Train or prompt an adversarial model to find flaws, alternative solutions, or counterexamples. Require its concession or its improvement.
2. **Blind-spot oracle**: Use a model specifically trained on "things humans miss" (e.g., trained on historical failure modes) to scan for missed considerations.
3. **Independent re-derivation**: Have a separate optimizer (different algorithm, different team, different model) attempt the same problem from scratch. Compare results.
4. **Rest-and-re-examine**: Built-in mandatory delay (hours for micro-tasks, weeks for macro-tasks) before declaration, with fresh re-evaluation upon return.
5. **Counter-optimization** (mandatory for ODI ≥ 7): See §3.7.

**Immortalization**: The final declaration is archived with:

- The full process trace (every stage, every iteration, every insight)
- The optimality certificate
- All raw evaluations and dimensional scores
- The context `C` and knowledge horizon `K` under which the declaration holds
- A re-verification schedule (when should this declaration be revisited?)

### 3.3 LAYER 2 — THE META-OPTIMIZATION LOOP

**Goal**: During execution of the blueprint, continuously monitor whether the current process is the optimal process, and adjust if not.

**Monitored Signals**:

- `Δ_n` — enhancement delta at iteration `n`
- `c_n` — certificate certainty at iteration `n`
- `g_n` — gap density (remaining gaps per unit solution-space volume)
- `r_n` — resource consumption rate
- `p_n` — predicted remaining gain (from theoretical limit)

**Adjustment Rules**:

1. **Stage insertion**: If a new gap source is detected that the current blueprint doesn't address, insert the corresponding transcendence operator.
2. **Stage removal**: If a stage's expected contribution to `U` is below threshold (given current state), skip it.
3. **Resource reallocation**: Reallocate budget from low-marginal-return stages to high-marginal-return stages.
4. **Rigor escalation**: If certificate certainty stalls below target, escalate to a stronger certification method (statistical → formal → redundant formal).
5. **Termination**: When `Δ_n < τ` AND `c_n ≥ c_target` AND `p_n < π` (residual predicted gain below threshold), terminate.

**The Meta-Optimization Loop is the online controller.** It is what makes ABSUBEST adaptive rather than static. `optibest` has no analog.

### 3.4 CONVERGANCE MATHEMATICS

(Read "Convergence" — heading retained for emphasis on the formal apparatus.)

Let:

- `x_n` = best solution found by iteration `n`
- `Δ_n = U(x_n) − U(x_{n−1})` = enhancement delta
- `U*` = `max_{x∈X̃} U(x)` (the contextual optimum)
- `δ_n = U* − U(x_n)` = residual gap (when `U*` is known or bounded)
- `c_n ∈ [0, 1]` = certificate certainty
- `ρ_n` = gap density (count of unaddressed gaps per unit volume of `X̃`)

**Convergence Criteria** (all required for declaration):

```
(C1) Δ_n < τ_Δ              [enhancement below threshold]
(C2) δ_n < τ_δ  OR  c_n ≥ 1−ε   [residual gap closed, or proof of optimality]
(C3) ρ_n < τ_ρ              [no dense unaddressed gaps]
(C4) All five plateau-verification methods confirm   [Phase 8 analog]
(C5) Counter-optimizer has conceded or been incorporated
```

Default thresholds (calibrated by ODI):

| ODI | τ_Δ | τ_δ | τ_ρ | ε |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0–2 | 0.1·U_scale | 0.2·U_scale | 0.5 | 0.20 |
| 3–5 | 0.05·U_scale | 0.1·U_scale | 0.2 | 0.05 |
| 6–8 | 0.01·U_scale | 0.05·U_scale | 0.05 | 0.01 |
| 9–10 | 0.001·U_scale | 0.01·U_scale | 0.01 | 0.001 |

**Convergence Rate**: Under directed recursion (§2.3.3), in the typical case `Δ_n` decreases geometrically: `Δ_n ≤ Δ_0 · r^n` for some `r < 1` determined by the structure of the gap decomposition. Termination is reached in `O(log(τ_Δ / Δ_0) / log(1/r))` iterations.

### 3.6 CROSS-DOMAIN INVARIANCE THEOREMS

**Theorem 1 (Universality)**: For any purpose `P` that satisfies Prerequisites P1–P8 (§2.2), ABSUBEST produces a declaration with the appropriate guarantee level.

*Proof*: Each prerequisite enables a specific stage. P1 → Stage A. P2 → Stage A utility construction. P3 → Stage D. P4 → Stage B. P5 → Stage F. P6 → Stage H. P7 → Meta-Calibrator budget. P8 → Declaration indexing. With all prerequisites met, all stages can execute, producing a declaration. ∎

**Theorem 2 (Optimality of Self-Adaptation)**: For any task `T`, the expected utility of ABSUBEST's adaptive blueprint is at least as high as the expected utility of any fixed framework `F`.

*Proof*: The blueprint space includes emulations of all fixed frameworks. The Meta-Calibrator selects the expected-utility-maximizing blueprint, which cannot be worse than any fixed point in the space. ∎

**Theorem 3 (Counter-Optimization Soundness)**: If the counter-optimizer is independent (shares no paradigm or blind spot with the primary optimizer) and concedes, then within the knowledge horizon `K`, no better solution exists *that is reachable by an optimizer with this paradigm independence*. This is weaker than absolute optimality but stronger than any non-counter-optimized claim.

*Proof*: The counter-optimizer is constructed to find a superior solution if one exists in the reachable space. Concession means no such solution was found. Independence ensures the counter-optimizer's reachable space is not a subset of the primary's blind spots. ∎

**Theorem 4 (Invariance Under Domain Translation)**: The structural form of ABSUBEST (Meta-Calibrator + eight stages + meta-loop + counter-optimization) is invariant across domains. Only the *content* of each stage (which dimensions, which evaluation methods, which solvers) varies.

*Argument*: The four traversal operations (GENERATE, EVALUATE, CERTIFY, TRANSCEND) are domain-agnostic. The ten core processes C1–C10 are domain-agnostic. The framework's structure is the composition of these; hence it is domain-agnostic. Domain content enters via Stage A's utility construction and Stage C's dimension derivation. ∎

### 3.7 COUNTER-OPTIMIZATION (Adversarial Phase)

Counter-optimization is mandatory for ODI ≥ 7 and recommended for all tasks. It is the constructive counterpart to `optibest`'s merely-critical "adversarial analysis."

**Protocol**:

1. **Spawn**: Instantiate an independent optimizer `O_adv` with:
   - The same purpose `P` and context `C`
   - The current best solution `x*` as the baseline to beat
   - A different paradigm where possible (different solver, different team, different model class)
   - Full independence from the primary optimizer's reasoning trace
2. **Charge**: `O_adv` must either (a) produce `x'` with `U(x') > U(x*)`, or (b) issue a formal concession stating that no such `x'` was found within its search.
3. **Time budget**: `O_adv` receives a meaningful fraction of the primary budget (default: 30%).
4. **Outcome handling**:
   - If `O_adv` produces superior `x'`: incorporate `x'` as the new `x*`; re-certify; re-counter-optimize.
   - If `O_adv` concedes: counter-optimization soundness (Theorem 3) is established.
   - If `O_adv` is inconclusive: declaration is downgraded to provisional; ODI is effectively capped at 6.
